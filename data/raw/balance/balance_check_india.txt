How to Check Bank Balance in India

OVERVIEW
You can check your bank account balance through branch, ATM, mobile app, internet banking, SMS, missed call service, passbook update, mini statement, or UPI.

METHOD 1: ATM BALANCE ENQUIRY
Step 1: Insert debit card at any ATM.
Step 2: Enter your 4-digit PIN.
Step 3: Select Balance Enquiry.
Step 4: Select account type, Savings or Current.
Step 5: Available balance is shown on screen.
Step 6: Optionally print a mini statement.

METHOD 2: MOBILE BANKING APP
Step 1: Download the bank official mobile app.
Step 2: Register using registered mobile number and debit card details.
Step 3: Set a 4 to 6 digit MPIN.
Step 4: Log in to the app.
Step 5: Balance is displayed on the home screen or Account Summary.
Step 6: Mini statement may show last 5 to 10 transactions.

METHOD 3: INTERNET BANKING
Step 1: Visit the bank official website.
Step 2: Click Net Banking or Login.
Step 3: Enter Customer ID or User ID and password.
Step 4: Dashboard shows balance under My Accounts.
Step 5: Click account to view statement or download PDF.

METHOD 4: MISSED CALL
- Register your mobile number with your bank account.
- Give a missed call to the bank balance enquiry number.
- The call disconnects automatically.
- You receive SMS with account balance.

Common missed call numbers must be verified with the bank because they may change:
- SBI: 9223766666
- HDFC: 1800-270-3333
- ICICI: 9594613613
- Axis Bank: 1800-419-6969
- Bank of Baroda: 8468001111
- Canara Bank: 09015483483
- PNB: 1800-180-2222

METHOD 5: SMS BANKING
Step 1: Register mobile number for SMS banking.
Step 2: Send SMS in bank-specified format, such as BAL followed by last 4 account digits.
Step 3: Receive SMS with current balance. Standard SMS charges may apply.

METHOD 6: PASSBOOK UPDATE
Step 1: Carry passbook to branch.
Step 2: Submit at passbook counter or use passbook kiosk.
Step 3: Passbook is updated with transactions and balance.

METHOD 7: MINI STATEMENT VIA ATM
Step 1: Insert card and enter PIN.
Step 2: Select Mini Statement.
Step 3: Printed slip shows recent transactions and balance.

UPI BALANCE CHECK
Step 1: Open any UPI app such as BHIM, GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm.
Step 2: Link bank account using debit card details.
Step 3: Select Check Balance or tap linked account.
Step 4: Enter UPI PIN.
Step 5: Balance is displayed instantly.

IMPORTANT NOTE
- Available Balance is the amount you can currently withdraw or use.
- Ledger Balance is total balance including pending transactions.
- Rely on Available Balance for withdrawals.
