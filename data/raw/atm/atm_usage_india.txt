How to Use an ATM in India

OVERVIEW
An ATM, or Automated Teller Machine, allows basic banking operations 24 hours a day without visiting a bank branch.

WHAT YOU NEED
- A valid debit card issued by your bank
- Your 4-digit ATM PIN

STEP-BY-STEP: CASH WITHDRAWAL
Step 1: Insert your debit card into the ATM card slot with the chip facing up.
Step 2: Select your preferred language.
Step 3: Enter your 4-digit PIN. Cover the keypad with your other hand for security.
Step 4: Select Withdrawal from the menu.
Step 5: Select your account type, Savings or Current.
Step 6: Enter the amount you wish to withdraw. Use multiples of Rs. 100 or Rs. 500.
Step 7: Confirm the amount when prompted.
Step 8: Collect cash from the cash dispenser.
Step 9: Collect printed receipt if requested.
Step 10: Remove your debit card. Some ATMs return the card before dispensing cash and some after dispensing cash.

STEP-BY-STEP: BALANCE ENQUIRY
Step 1: Insert card and enter PIN.
Step 2: Select Balance Enquiry.
Step 3: Select account type.
Step 4: Available balance is displayed on screen.
Step 5: Choose to print a mini statement if needed.

STEP-BY-STEP: PIN CHANGE
Step 1: Insert card and enter current PIN.
Step 2: Select PIN Change or Other Services.
Step 3: Enter current PIN when prompted.
Step 4: Enter the new 4-digit PIN.
Step 5: Re-enter the new PIN to confirm.
Step 6: PIN is changed successfully.

DAILY WITHDRAWAL LIMITS
- Most banks allow Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 25,000 per day from savings accounts.
- Premium accounts may have limits up to Rs. 1,00,000 per day.
- Limits vary by bank and account type.

FREE TRANSACTION LIMITS
- 5 free transactions per month at your own bank ATMs.
- 3 free transactions per month at other bank ATMs in metro cities such as Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai, Kolkata, Bengaluru, and Hyderabad.
- 5 free transactions per month at other bank ATMs in non-metro cities.
- After the free limit, charges may apply. RBI revised financial transaction charges to Rs. 21 from January 2022.

SAFETY TIPS
- Never share your PIN with anyone, including bank staff.
- Shield the keypad when entering your PIN.
- Do not accept help from strangers at the ATM.
- Always collect your card before leaving.
- If the ATM retains your card, call your bank helpline immediately and block the card.
- If cash is not dispensed but amount is debited, note the ATM ID, date, and time, then complain to your bank. Refund timelines follow RBI rules.

WHAT TO DO IF ATM SWALLOWS YOUR CARD
Step 1: Do not leave the ATM immediately.
Step 2: Note the ATM ID number displayed on the machine.
Step 3: Call the bank 24x7 helpline and block the card.
Step 4: Visit the branch next working day with identity proof to request replacement.
