How to File a Complaint Against a Bank in India

OVERVIEW
If you face problems such as wrong charges, failed transactions, poor service, or fraud, RBI provides a complaint escalation path.

STEP 1: COMPLAIN TO YOUR BANK FIRST
All complaints must first be raised with the bank. Banks are required to resolve complaints within prescribed timelines.

Methods:
- Branch: Visit branch and speak to Branch Manager. Request written acknowledgement.
- Customer Care: Call 24x7 helpline and note complaint reference number.
- Net Banking or Mobile App: Use Raise Complaint or Service Request.
- Email: Send complaint to official customer care email.
- Written Letter: Write to Branch Manager or Nodal Officer.

What to include:
- Full name and account number
- Nature of problem
- Date and amount of transaction
- Reference or transaction ID
- Expected resolution

STEP 2: ESCALATE TO NODAL OFFICER
If bank does not resolve within 30 days, escalate to bank Nodal Officer. Banks publish Nodal Officer contact details.

STEP 3: RBI INTEGRATED OMBUDSMAN SCHEME
File complaint with RBI Ombudsman if:
- Bank does not resolve complaint within 30 days.
- Bank rejects complaint.
- You are not satisfied with bank response.

IMPORTANT
You must approach the bank first. Ombudsman may not entertain complaint without proof of bank complaint.

HOW TO FILE WITH RBI OMBUDSMAN ONLINE
Step 1: Visit cms.rbi.org.in.
Step 2: Click File a Complaint.
Step 3: Enter name, address, and contact details.
Step 4: Select bank and complaint type.
Step 5: Describe complaint.
Step 6: Attach supporting documents.
Step 7: Submit and note complaint registration number.

HOW TO FILE BY POST
Write to the RBI Ombudsman at the regional office for your state.

COMPLAINTS COVERED
- Failure to credit funds
- Delay in cheque payment
- Refusal to close account without valid reason
- ATM transaction failure and non-refund
- Unauthorised debit or fraud
- Wrong charges or fees
- Non-return of property documents after loan repayment
- Failure to issue KYC acknowledgement
- UPI, NEFT, or RTGS failures

ATM TRANSACTION FAILURE RULE
If ATM withdrawal fails but money is debited:
Step 1: Report immediately to bank with ATM location, date, time, and amount.
Step 2: Bank must refund within required RBI timeline.
Step 3: Delay beyond timeline may require compensation.

FAILED UPI, NEFT, OR RTGS
- UPI failures should normally auto-reverse quickly.
- NEFT and RTGS failures have defined reversal timelines.
- If reversal does not happen, complain to bank and escalate where required.

USEFUL CONTACTS
- RBI Helpline: 14448
- RBI Complaint Portal: cms.rbi.org.in
- NPCI support for UPI issues: npci.org.in
- Cyber Crime Complaints: cybercrime.gov.in or 1930

SOURCE
RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme 2021, RBI ATM refund circulars, Consumer Protection Act 2019, and NPCI dispute resolution guidelines.
