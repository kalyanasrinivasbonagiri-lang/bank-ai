Bank Form Field Explanations

OVERVIEW
This document explains common fields on deposit slips, withdrawal slips, cheque leaves, and demand draft request forms.

PART 1: CASH DEPOSIT SLIP FIELDS

Date
The date on which you make the deposit. Use DD/MM/YYYY format.

Branch Name
The bank branch where you are making the deposit.

Account Number
Your 11 to 16-digit bank account number where money should be credited. Write clearly without spaces.

Account Holder Name
Name of the person or entity as registered with the bank. Write exactly as shown in passbook.

Amount in Figures
Exact cash amount in numbers, such as 5000.00.

Amount in Words
Full amount in words, such as Five Thousand Rupees Only. Always end with Only.

Denomination Details
A table where you fill number of notes of each denomination, such as Rs. 500, Rs. 200, Rs. 100, Rs. 50, Rs. 20, Rs. 10, coins, and total.

Depositor Signature
Signature confirming that deposit is intentional.

Depositor Mobile Number
Some banks ask for mobile number to send SMS confirmation.

PART 2: CHEQUE DEPOSIT SLIP FIELDS
Cheque deposit slips include the cash deposit fields plus:

Cheque Number
The 6-digit number printed at bottom left of cheque leaf in the MICR area.

Cheque Date
The date written on the cheque by the drawer.

Drawn on Bank and Branch
The bank and branch on which the cheque is issued.

IFSC Code
The 11-character code identifying the bank and branch for electronic transactions. Sometimes required.

PART 3: WITHDRAWAL SLIP FIELDS

Date
Date of withdrawal.

Account Number
Account from which money is to be withdrawn.

Account Holder Name
Name as per bank records.

Amount in Figures and Words
Exact amount to withdraw.

Purpose
Reason for withdrawal, such as Personal Use, Medical, or Travel. Optional at most banks.

Signature
Must match the signature on record with the bank.

Token Number
Given by token counter or system to track queue transaction.

PART 4: CHEQUE LEAF FIELDS

Date
Write the current date. Post-dated cheques are valid but cannot be encashed before date.

Pay or Payee Name
Write the person or company who should receive the money. Write Self to withdraw for yourself.

Rupees or Amount in Words
Write full amount in words followed by Only. Draw a line after words to prevent fraud.

Amount in Figures Box
Write the amount in numbers in the box.

A/c Payee Only Crossing
Draw two parallel lines at top left and write A/c Payee between them. This means cheque can only be deposited into the payee bank account, not encashed at counter.

Signature
Sign at bottom right. Signature must match bank record. Use blue or black ink.

Cheque Number, MICR Code, Account Number, IFSC
These are pre-printed at bottom of cheque. Do not write over MICR band.

PART 5: DEMAND DRAFT REQUEST FORM FIELDS

Applicant Name
Name of person requesting DD.

Applicant Account Number
Account from which DD amount will be debited.

Payee Name
Name of person or company to whom DD is payable.

Payable At
City or branch where DD is to be paid.

Amount
Amount in figures and words.

SOURCE
RBI Master Circular on Cheque Operations, NPCI CTS guidelines, and bank form guides.
