How to Transfer Money Between Bank Accounts in India

OVERVIEW
India offers multiple fund transfer systems. Choose method based on amount, urgency, and time of transfer.

TRANSFER METHOD COMPARISON
- NEFT: No minimum, no RBI maximum, batch processing around every 30 minutes, available 24x7, free online.
- RTGS: Minimum Rs. 2 lakh, no RBI maximum, real-time settlement, available 24x7, free online.
- IMPS: Minimum Re. 1, maximum usually Rs. 5 lakh, instant, available 24x7 including holidays, small fee may apply.
- UPI: Minimum Re. 1, general maximum Rs. 1 lakh per transaction, instant, available 24x7, usually free.

METHOD 1: NEFT
Use NEFT for transfers of any amount where instant credit is not required.

HOW TO DO NEFT ONLINE
Step 1: Log in to internet banking or mobile banking.
Step 2: Go to Fund Transfer or Pay.
Step 3: Add beneficiary by entering account number, account type, IFSC code, and name.
Step 4: Wait for beneficiary activation if required.
Step 5: Select beneficiary and choose NEFT.
Step 6: Enter amount and remarks.
Step 7: Confirm using OTP.
Step 8: Transaction is queued for next NEFT batch.

HOW TO DO NEFT AT BRANCH
Step 1: Fill NEFT or RTGS request form.
Step 2: Provide sender account number, beneficiary account number, IFSC, beneficiary name, and amount.
Step 3: Sign and submit at counter.

METHOD 2: RTGS
Use RTGS for high-value transfers of Rs. 2 lakh and above when funds must be credited immediately. Process is similar to NEFT, but select RTGS instead.

RTGS DETAILS
- Minimum amount: Rs. 2,00,000.
- No RBI maximum limit, though banks may set internal caps.
- Settlement is real-time.
- Available 24x7 since December 14, 2020.

DELAY PENALTY
If RTGS or NEFT transaction is delayed beyond stipulated time, bank must compensate as per RBI guidelines.

BENEFICIARY NAME VERIFICATION
RBI introduced beneficiary name verification for NEFT and RTGS to help reduce fraud and wrong transfers.

METHOD 3: IMPS
Use IMPS for instant transfers at any time, including nights, weekends, and public holidays.

HOW TO DO IMPS ONLINE
Step 1: Log in to mobile banking or net banking.
Step 2: Go to Fund Transfer and select IMPS.
Step 3: Enter beneficiary account number and IFSC, or use mobile number plus MMID.
Step 4: Enter amount and confirm with OTP.
Step 5: Funds are credited instantly.

METHOD 4: UPI
Use UPI for instant small to medium payments and transfers.

HOW TO DO UPI TRANSFER
Step 1: Open UPI app such as BHIM, PhonePe, GPay, Paytm, or bank app.
Step 2: Tap Send Money or Pay.
Step 3: Enter UPI ID, mobile number, or scan QR code.
Step 4: Enter amount and optional remark.
Step 5: Enter UPI PIN.
Step 6: Money is transferred instantly.

UPI LIMITS
- General UPI transaction limit is up to Rs. 1 lakh.
- Specific categories such as medical, education, or IPO may allow higher limits.
- UPI Lite supports small offline transactions.

WHAT IS IFSC CODE
IFSC is an 11-character code identifying a bank branch. Example: SBIN0001234. It is printed on cheque book, passbook, and may be searched through RBI IFSC resources.

IMPORTANT NOTES
- Verify beneficiary account number and IFSC before confirming transfer.
- Use beneficiary name verification where available.
- Never transfer money to unknown persons based on calls or messages claiming to be bank officials.
- Keep transaction reference number as proof.

SOURCE
RBI NEFT and RTGS guidelines, NPCI IMPS and UPI guidelines, and RBI circulars.
