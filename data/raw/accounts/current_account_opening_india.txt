How to Open a Current Account in India

OVERVIEW
A current account is designed for businesses, firms, and high-transaction users. Unlike a savings account, it usually does not earn interest but allows frequent transactions and overdraft facilities.

WHO SHOULD OPEN IT
- Business owners and sole proprietors
- Partnership firms, companies, and trusts
- Professionals with high volume transactions such as doctors, lawyers, and traders

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS
Identity Proof of account holder:
- Aadhaar Card
- PAN Card
- Passport

Address Proof of account holder:
- Utility bill
- Aadhaar Card
- Bank statement

Business Proof, any one:
- GST Registration Certificate
- Shop and Establishment Certificate
- Trade Licence
- Partnership Deed for partnership firms
- Certificate of Incorporation for companies
- Udyam Registration for MSMEs

Other requirements:
- PAN Card of the business entity is mandatory.
- Photographs of all authorised signatories are required.

STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS
Step 1: Visit the branch and inform staff that you need a current account.
Step 2: Collect and fill the Account Opening Form for current accounts.
Step 3: Provide all business documents and personal KYC of authorised signatories.
Step 4: Submit the initial deposit, typically Rs. 5000 to Rs. 25000 depending on bank and account type.
Step 5: Bank verifies documents and may call to confirm business details.
Step 6: Account is activated within 1 to 3 working days.
Step 7: Collect cheque book, debit card, and internet banking credentials.

MINIMUM BALANCE
- Current accounts require a higher minimum balance than savings accounts, typically Rs. 5000 to Rs. 25000.
- Penalty applies if balance falls below the required minimum.

OVERDRAFT FACILITY
- Current account holders can apply for an overdraft, also called OD, facility.
- OD allows withdrawing more than the available balance up to a sanctioned limit.
- Interest is charged only on the amount used and number of days used.
