How to Open a Savings Account in India

OVERVIEW
A savings account is the most basic bank account that allows you to deposit money, earn interest, and access funds anytime. Any Indian resident can open one at a bank branch or online.

ELIGIBILITY
- Must be 18 years or older. Minors can open jointly with a guardian.
- Must be an Indian resident or NRI. Different rules apply for NRIs.
- Valid KYC documents are required.

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS
Identity Proof, any one:
- Aadhaar Card
- PAN Card
- Passport
- Voter ID
- Driving Licence

Address Proof, any one:
- Aadhaar Card
- Utility bill such as electricity, water, or gas, not older than 3 months
- Passport
- Bank statement from another bank

Other documents:
- 2 recent passport-size colour photographs
- PAN Card, mandatory for most banks above a certain balance

STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS AT BRANCH
Step 1: Visit your nearest bank branch during working hours. Typical timings are 10 AM to 4 PM, Monday to Friday, and Saturday 10 AM to 2 PM.
Step 2: Request an Account Opening Form, also called AOF, from the counter staff.
Step 3: Fill in the form with your full name, date of birth, address, nominee details, mobile number, and email ID.
Step 4: Attach self-attested copies of your ID proof, address proof, and photographs.
Step 5: Submit the form and documents to the bank officer.
Step 6: Complete KYC verification. The officer will verify your original documents.
Step 7: Biometric data such as fingerprint may be captured at the branch.
Step 8: Submit the initial deposit amount. This varies by bank, usually Rs. 500 to Rs. 1000 for regular savings accounts. Zero-balance accounts are also available under PM Jan Dhan Yojana.
Step 9: Receive your account number, passbook, and debit card application acknowledgment.

ONLINE ACCOUNT OPENING WITH VIDEO KYC
Step 1: Visit the bank website or mobile app.
Step 2: Click Open Account or Apply Now.
Step 3: Enter your Aadhaar and PAN details.
Step 4: Verify your mobile number with OTP.
Step 5: Complete Video KYC. A bank officer conducts a short video call to verify identity.
Step 6: Account is activated digitally. Passbook and debit card are sent by post.

TYPES OF SAVINGS ACCOUNTS
- Regular Savings Account: Standard account with minimum balance requirement.
- Zero Balance Account, also called BSBD or Jan Dhan: No minimum balance needed, with basic facilities.
- Women's Savings Account: Extra benefits such as higher interest or cashback for women.
- Senior Citizen Account: Higher interest rates and priority service for persons above 60.
- Salary Account: Opened by employers, with no minimum balance required.

INITIAL DEPOSIT
- Regular savings: Rs. 500 to Rs. 5000, varying by bank and branch.
- Jan Dhan or BSBD accounts: Zero initial deposit required.

INTEREST RATE
- Typically 2.5% to 4% per annum on savings accounts.
- Calculated daily on end-of-day balance and credited quarterly or half-yearly.
- Rates vary by bank and are subject to change as per RBI policy.

IMPORTANT NOTES
- Always carry original documents along with self-attested photocopies.
- PAN Card is mandatory if annual cash deposits exceed Rs. 10 lakh.
- If PAN is not available, fill Form 60.
- Minor accounts below 18 can be opened jointly with a natural guardian.
- NRIs should ask about NRE or NRO account options separately.
