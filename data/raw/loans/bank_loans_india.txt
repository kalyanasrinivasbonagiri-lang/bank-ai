How to Apply for a Bank Loan in India

OVERVIEW
Banks in India offer many loan types. This guide covers Personal Loan, Home Loan, and Gold Loan.

PART 1: PERSONAL LOAN

WHAT IS IT
A personal loan is an unsecured loan based on income and credit history. No collateral is required.

ELIGIBILITY
- Age usually 21 to 60 years.
- Salaried employment with minimum work experience, or self-employed with business history.
- Minimum monthly income varies by bank and city.
- Credit score of 700 or above is recommended.

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED
- Aadhaar Card and PAN Card
- Last 3 months salary slips for salaried applicants or last 2 years ITR for self-employed applicants
- Last 6 months bank statements
- Employment proof
- Passport-size photograph

STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS
Step 1: Check eligibility and calculate EMI.
Step 2: Apply online or at branch.
Step 3: Fill loan application form.
Step 4: Submit required documents.
Step 5: Bank verifies documents and checks CIBIL score.
Step 6: Loan is approved or rejected.
Step 7: Receive sanction letter with amount, interest rate, tenure, and EMI.
Step 8: Sign loan agreement.
Step 9: Amount is disbursed to bank account.

PART 2: HOME LOAN

WHAT IS IT
A loan to purchase or construct property. Property acts as collateral.

ELIGIBILITY
- Age usually 21 to 65 years.
- Credit score of 750 or above is recommended.
- Stable income for salaried or self-employed applicants.
- Property must meet bank requirements.

LOAN-TO-VALUE RATIO
- Home loans up to Rs. 30 lakh: maximum 90% LTV.
- Rs. 30 lakh to Rs. 75 lakh: maximum 80% LTV.
- Above Rs. 75 lakh: maximum 75% LTV.

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED
- Aadhaar, PAN, Passport
- Salary slips or ITR
- Bank statements
- Property documents such as sale agreement, title deed, approved plan
- NOC from builder or society where applicable

STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS
Step 1: Calculate eligible loan amount and EMI.
Step 2: Apply at branch or online.
Step 3: Submit personal, income, and property documents.
Step 4: Bank conducts valuation and legal check.
Step 5: Loan is sanctioned.
Step 6: Sign loan agreement and mortgage deed.
Step 7: Loan is disbursed to seller or builder account as per process.

HOME LOAN RULES
- Interest is charged from actual disbursement date.
- Floating-rate loans are linked to external benchmark.
- No prepayment penalty on floating-rate home loans.
- Banks must return original property documents within required timeline after full repayment.

PART 3: GOLD LOAN

WHAT IS IT
A secured loan where gold jewellery or coins are pledged as collateral.

ELIGIBILITY
- Individual with valid KYC documents.
- Applicant must own the gold.
- Gold accepted is usually jewellery and ornaments of accepted purity.

STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS
Step 1: Visit branch with gold and KYC documents.
Step 2: Bank officer tests purity.
Step 3: Purity certificate or valuation details are issued.
Step 4: Loan amount is calculated based on loan-to-value ratio.
Step 5: Sign loan agreement.
Step 6: Amount is credited or given as per bank process.
Step 7: Repay loan with interest within agreed tenure.
Step 8: Bank returns gold after full repayment.

IMPORTANT GOLD LOAN NOTES
- Gold loan proceeds should not be used to purchase more gold.
- Bullet repayment loans usually have limited tenure.
- Ask bank for exact LTV and repayment terms.
