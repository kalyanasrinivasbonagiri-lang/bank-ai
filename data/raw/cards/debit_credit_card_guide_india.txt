Debit and Credit Card Guide for Indian Bank Customers

OVERVIEW
This guide explains how to apply for, use, block, and manage debit and credit cards.

PART 1: DEBIT CARD

WHAT IS A DEBIT CARD
A debit card is linked directly to a bank account. When you pay or withdraw, amount is deducted from account balance.

HOW TO GET A DEBIT CARD
Step 1: Debit card is usually issued when opening savings or current account.
Step 2: If not issued automatically, request one at branch.
Step 3: Fill debit card request form.
Step 4: Card is delivered by post within 7 to 10 working days.
Step 5: Activate card by generating PIN at ATM or mobile app.

NOTE
Banks cannot force a customer to take a debit card. It is optional.

DEBIT CARD PIN GENERATION AT ATM
Step 1: Insert card at ATM.
Step 2: Select PIN Generation or PIN Change.
Step 3: Enter registered mobile OTP if prompted.
Step 4: Set new 4-digit PIN.

DEBIT CARD PIN GENERATION IN MOBILE APP
Step 1: Log in to bank app.
Step 2: Go to Cards or Debit Card Services.
Step 3: Select Generate PIN or Set PIN.
Step 4: Authenticate with OTP.
Step 5: Set new PIN.

DAILY TRANSACTION LIMITS
- ATM cash withdrawal: Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 1,00,000 per day depending on card and bank.
- POS transactions: Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 2,00,000 per day.
- Online transactions: Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 1,00,000 per day.

PART 2: CREDIT CARD

WHAT IS A CREDIT CARD
A credit card allows purchases on credit up to a pre-approved limit. Used amount is repaid by monthly due date.

HOW TO APPLY
Step 1: Check eligibility. Minimum income and good credit score are usually required.
Step 2: Apply online, through mobile app, or branch.
Step 3: Submit Aadhaar, PAN, salary slips or ITR, and photograph.
Step 4: Bank checks CIBIL score and income.
Step 5: Credit card is approved and issued within 7 to 15 working days.

NOTE
Banks cannot issue a credit card without explicit consent. Unsolicited cards are prohibited.

BILLING CYCLE
- Billing cycle is usually 30 days.
- Statement shows transactions and total due.
- Payment is due within 15 to 20 days of statement date.
- Pay full outstanding to avoid interest.
- Minimum Amount Due avoids late fee but interest applies on unpaid amount.

PART 3: BLOCKING LOST OR STOLEN CARD

Mobile app method:
Step 1: Open bank app.
Step 2: Go to Cards.
Step 3: Select Block Card or Hot-list Card.
Step 4: Confirm block.

Customer care method:
Step 1: Call 24x7 bank helpline.
Step 2: Select card block option.
Step 3: Verify identity.
Step 4: Card is blocked immediately.

Net banking method:
Step 1: Log in to net banking.
Step 2: Go to Card Services.
Step 3: Select Block Card and confirm.

SMS method:
Send SMS in the format specified by the bank.

IMPORTANT
Block card first, then visit branch or app to apply for replacement card.

PART 4: REPORTING UNAUTHORISED TRANSACTIONS
Step 1: Immediately call bank helpline or block card.
Step 2: Lodge complaint within 3 working days of noticing transaction.
Step 3: If reported within 3 days, RBI zero-liability framework may apply where customer is not negligent.
Step 4: Bank must resolve complaint and credit amount within required timeline.
Step 5: If unresolved, escalate to RBI Ombudsman.

PART 5: CARD SAFETY TIPS
- Never share card number, CVV, PIN, or OTP.
- Cover keypad at ATMs and POS machines.
- Register transaction alerts.
- Use virtual cards for online transactions if available.
- Check statement regularly.

SOURCE
RBI Master Directions on Credit Card and Debit Card Issuance and Conduct, RBI card FAQs, and RBI electronic transaction liability framework.
