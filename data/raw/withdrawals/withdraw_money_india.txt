How to Withdraw Money from a Bank in India

OVERVIEW
You can withdraw money from a bank account at branch counter, ATM, or through digital payment methods. This document covers counter withdrawals and required documents.

METHOD 1: CASH WITHDRAWAL AT BRANCH COUNTER

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS
- Filled withdrawal slip
- Original passbook or account statement
- Valid government-issued photo ID such as Aadhaar, Voter ID, Driving Licence, or PAN
- Signature must match bank records

STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS
Step 1: Collect withdrawal slip from counter or slip rack.
Step 2: Fill date, account number, account holder name, amount in figures, amount in words, and purpose if asked.
Step 3: Sign withdrawal slip. Signature must match the bank record.
Step 4: Carry passbook and one valid photo ID.
Step 5: Submit slip and documents at cash withdrawal counter.
Step 6: Teller verifies signature and account details.
Step 7: For large amounts, generally above Rs. 50,000, teller may ask for PAN card.
Step 8: Receive cash and count it at counter before leaving.
Step 9: Passbook is updated or stamped receipt may be issued.

LARGE CASH WITHDRAWALS
- Withdrawals of Rs. 50,000 or above require PAN Card as per Income Tax Rules.
- If PAN is not available, submit Form 60.
- Withdrawals above Rs. 2 lakh in cash in a single day may be subject to TDS under Section 194N if total cash withdrawal in financial year exceeds specified threshold.

METHOD 2: ATM WITHDRAWAL
- Use ATM guide for full steps.
- Maximum per transaction is often Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 25,000.
- Daily limit may range from Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 1,00,000 depending on account and card.

METHOD 3: CHEQUE SELF-WITHDRAWAL
Step 1: Write cheque in your own name or write Self in Pay field.
Step 2: Write amount in words and figures.
Step 3: Date and sign cheque.
Step 4: Submit cheque at cash payment counter of your own branch.
Step 5: Teller verifies signature and available balance.
Step 6: Collect cash.

IMPORTANT NOTES
- Branch counter withdrawal requires signature match.
- If signature changed, update it with bank.
- Third-party withdrawals require authorisation letter and ID proof of both parties.
- Banks may impose cash handling charges above certain limits.
- Count cash at counter. Complaints about wrong amount may not be accepted after leaving.
- Cash transaction timings may be shorter than branch hours.

SOURCE
